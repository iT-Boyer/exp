D&D 4th Edition on your iPhone

by Jay McCarthy on 2008/06/10

In the past week, two amazing products came on my radar. First, the new 4th edition of Dungeons and Dragons was released by Wizards of the Coast. Second, the new iPhone 3G was announced. I thought, why not have these two great things together?

Part of the new D&D effort is Dungeons and Dragons Insider (DDI), a way of enabling a more digital and enhanced experience on the tabletop. I'm very excited about the Character Builder that helps when designing a D&D character. Unfortunately, the Builder is only advertised to produce a nice character sheet for the table.

While thinking about this Sunday (2008/06/08), I read a post from Ken Tropp, the executive producer of DDI, and put in my request for an iPhone interface. After the Sabbath, on Monday (2008/06/09), I decided to do something about it and make my own.

Let me take you on a tour. (Click on any image for a zoom-in.)

On the top, we have the name of the character and the 'Status' button, with the current HP. Each of the links goes to a different section of the character sheet.

The first link goes to the basic statistics about the character. One thing to notice at this point is that this is meant to be a character sheet viewer and combat supplement. I didn't design it to be the sheet where you, e.g., calculate where modifiers come from, etc.

Notice that we can go back to the main page on the upper left and that the Status button is always there on the upper right.

The second link is the features view, where you can record free-form information about the race, class, paragon, etc. features of a character.

The third link is the skills view. Every skill, with its core ability and modifier is listed. (Again, it does not say where these come from. In Kugo's case, this might be relevant, because he uses a shield which gives Check penalties.) One nice thing about this view is that Insight and Perception on "above the fold", so you can easily get their Passive values.

The fourth link down is the feats list. When you click on a feat, you can view that feat's details:

Let's skip down to the sixth link, before looking at Powers and the Status button.

Sixth is the Inventory view. Currently, a simple text area. I haven't played long enough to know what capabilities this part should have.

The coolest part of the sheet is the Powers and Status section.

Your powers are divided up into At-will, Encounter, and Daily sections. Clicking on any power will bring up its card.

The display shows the character's defenses and HP. Since the button is always there, these are easily accessible during play.

At the bottom of the Status view are two buttons:

We will get to the buttons in a moment. Suppose we need to record damage, we just click on the HP field and type in a new value.

If the new value is less than half the max, then the character is bloodied and the Status button changes from Blue to Red.

More Details

The application is not tied to any database, so everything must be typed in manually, although there is a lot of structure to the code, so it is pretty simple for an amateur to deal with it. Ideally, I'd like to make something that exports a custom sheet from some other program, such as the Character Builder or something else.

The application is a single HTML page. This is useful because there is literally no lag when looking at the different parts of the sheet. One side-effect of this is that the sheet has no memory. If you press Refresh, then the HP, power usage, etc. is reset.

I wrote this in one day using Joe Hewitt's iUI library, the Flapjax language, the iPhoney iPhone simulator, and GNU Emacs.
